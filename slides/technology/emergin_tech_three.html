What is the difference between Morals and Ethics?

Morals vs. Ethics

Morals: Morals are one's personal beliefs about right and wrong. These beliefs are generally influenced by religion, culture, social status, and upbringing.

Ethics: Ethics are shaped by society, industry, and professional codes

Moral Examples:

  • Feeling personally responsible for protecting user data even when not legally required
  • Believing it’s wrong to profit from biased algorithms
  • Choosing not to participate in projects you feel are unethical, even if they're legal
  • Believing users should always control their own digital footprint

Ethical Examples:

  • Designing AI systems to avoid racial or gender bias
  • Disclosing security breaches to users and regulators
  • Implementing user consent for data collection
  • Following accessibility standards (e.g., WCAG compliance)

Core Ethical Principles

How does this relate to the system we build and use that utilize emerging technology?

Privacy
Ensuring that data is not misused and that specific identities of individuals are protected when appropriate.
Consider: How data is collected and who has access

Accuracy
Be aware of the state of the data and eliminate any inaccurate and incomplete data.
Consider:Eliminate Fake/Duplicate data, encourage verification

Property (primarily Intellectual Property)
Consider the ethical issues that arise when it comes to intellectual property.
Consider:Gaining clarity and documentation

Accessibility
Consider: Where the data is located, whether the individuals have access to their personal data, and whether they can opt out or opt in to various programs

Ethical Framework for Information Technology Decision‐Making

This framework can also be applied to decision‐making for emerging technologies

Identify
When making a decision, managers should first consider which ethical principles are at stake, who will be directly or indirectly affected by the decision, whether there is a conflict of interest, and the facts about the technology.

Consider
Managers should take time to assess the alternatives and evaluate the possible outcomes of the decision. Here managers should be aware of how information overload may influence their evaluation.

Act
When carrying out the chosen alternative, managers should assign responsibility and ensure that every employee is aware of their responsibilities.

Reflect
Evaluate the implemented processes and outcomes to identify any ethical issues.

Social Responsibility of Emerging Technology

Social Responsibility of Emerging Technology

Digital Inclusion


Ensuring that everyone has access to and can benefit from emerging technologies is crucial for social equity.

Social Impact


Technology can be used to address social problems, but it can also exacerbate inequalities.

Environmental Sustainability


Emerging technologies offer tools for reducing environmental impact, such as green energy solutions and more efficient resource management.

Innovation for Social Good


Emerging technologies can be used to innovate in CSR, creating new ways to address social and environmental challenges. This could involve developing new products and services, improving supply chains, or leveraging data for social impact.

Transparency and Accountability


Companies should be transparent about how they are using emerging technologies and accountable for their social and environmental impact.

Ethical Considerations


Emerging technologies raise ethical dilemmas related to data privacy, algorithmic bias, and the potential for misuse. Companies must prioritize ethical development and deployment, ensuring transparency and accountability.
