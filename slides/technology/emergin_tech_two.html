User Experience Research

Why User research?

A few User Research methods

Observations
Give people tasks or instructions and watch them use your design, without help.

Interviews
Ask prospective users questions about their expectations of your design.

Task Analysis
learning about user goals, and understand the tasks users will perform on your site.

Focus Groups
a moderated discussion where you can learn about users’ attitudes, beliefs, desires, and reactions to concepts.

Surveys
a structured questionnaire that your target audience completes over the internet generally through a filling out a form

Industry Analysis Learning Activity (Group) - Gartner Hype Cycle

Access Gartner + Locate Hype Cycle
1. Watch the two videos to the left and access your Gartner Account via libraries.luc.edu
2. Locate the Hype Cycle tab and search for the Emerging Technologies 2024 hype cycle.

Review Hype Cycle
1. As a group, agree and identify one technology in each of the "innovation trigger", "Peak of Inflated Expectations", and "Trough of Disillusionment" timeline.
2. Click on the respective technologies to read more about the technology.

Analyze Hype Cycle
1. Have a brief discussion about the technology
2. Conduct an online search to identify a company that is implementing this technology.
3. Identify the time to plateau, market penetration, maturity, and benefit rating
4. Identify someone to provide a brief summary of technologies reviewed.
